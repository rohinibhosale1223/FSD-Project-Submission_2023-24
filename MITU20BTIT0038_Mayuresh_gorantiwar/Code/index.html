Indian Dishes:

  • Butter Chicken

    Ingredients:
    - 500g boneless chicken
    - 2 tablespoons butter
    - 1 onion, finely chopped
    - 2 tomatoes, pureed
    - 1/2 cup yogurt
    - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    - 1 teaspoon garam masala
    - 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    - 1/2 cup cream
    - Salt to taste

    Instructions:
    1. Heat butter in a pan and sauté onions until golden.
    2. Add ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, red chili powder, and turmeric powder. Cook for a minute.
    3. Add chicken pieces and cook until they are no longer pink.
    4. Stir in tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
    5. Add yogurt and simmer for a few minutes.
    6. Stir in cream and cook until the chicken is fully cooked.
    7. Serve hot with naan or rice.

  • Palak Paneer

    Ingredients:
    - 200g paneer (cottage cheese)
    - 2 bunches of spinach
    - 1 onion, finely chopped
    - 2 tomatoes, pureed
    - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    - 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    - 1/2 cup cream
    - Salt to taste

    Instructions:
    1. Blanch spinach in boiling water, then blend into a smooth puree.
    2. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and sauté onions until golden.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
    4. Stir in tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
    5. Add turmeric powder, garam masala, and spinach puree. Cook for a few minutes.
    6. Add paneer and simmer until it absorbs the flavors.
    7. Stir in cream and serve hot with naan or rice.

  • Chana Masala

    Ingredients:
    - 1 cup dried chickpeas
    - 1 large onion, finely chopped
    - 2 tomatoes, chopped
    - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    - 1 green chili, finely chopped
    - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    - 1 teaspoon garam masala
    - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    - 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    - 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
    - 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
    - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
    - Salt to taste

    Instructions:
    1. Soak chickpeas overnight, then boil until tender. Drain and set aside.
    2. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and sauté onions until golden.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili, and cook for a minute.
    4. Stir in chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
    5. Add garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt.
    6. Mix well and add boiled chickpeas. Cook for a few minutes.
    7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve with rice or bhature.

  • Gulab Jamun

    Ingredients:
    - 1 cup milk powder
    - 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
    - 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
    - 1/4 cup ghee or melted butter
    - 1/4 cup milk
    - A pinch of cardamom powder
    - Saffron strands for garnish
    - Sugar syrup (1 cup sugar and 1 cup water, boiled)

    Instructions:
    1. Mix milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking powder.
    2. Add ghee/butter and milk. Knead into a soft dough.
    3. Divide the dough into small balls and shape them into smooth balls.
    4. Heat oil/ghee in a pan and fry the balls until golden brown.
    5. Soak the fried balls in hot sugar syrup for a few hours.
    6. Garnish with saffron strands and serve.
